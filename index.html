I model and combine different probes of large-scale structure in our Universe to gain insights into gravity, Dark Energy, and structure formation. I conduct my research in collaboration with Prof. Dragan Huterer's group at the Physics Department, University of Michigan -
where I work as a Leinweber Research Fellow. A summary of my academic and professional background can be found in my CV.

I enjoy collaboration as much as research. I am a member of the MPA LEFTfield group, the Aquila consortium and the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI) collaboration.

I love doing public outreach and have, for example, participated in the "Skype a Scientist" program. I firmly believe in science popularization as a mean to improve diversity and inclusivity in science. In 2021, I inititaed and have since been organizing the (virtual) VLLT Joint Seminar Series on Physics & Astrophysics, geared towards students in Vietnam. I describe these activities further in SOCIAL.

I was a PhD student at the Max Planck Institute for Astrophysics (MPA) in Garching, Germany. There I have worked with Fabian Schmidt on Bayesian forward modeling of galaxy clustering and its application to cosmological or astrophysical inference.

I obtained my PhD through the IMPRS-astro program, a partnership between the Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität München and astro institutes around Munich, in July 2020 amid the height of the COVID-19 pandemic. We had to wait a few months to celebrate my graduation (hence the distance between people in the above picture). I stayed in Munich for another year and a half to wait for the pandemic to calm down and also to resolve some visa issues.

Before MPA and Munich, from 2014 to 2016, I was an AstroMundus master student studying in Innsbruck, Rome and Göttingen. I wrote my Master thesis with Thorsten Battefeld on stabilizing numerical construction of the inflaton potential in multifield inflationary models.

Even before that, I completed my bachelor in Physics, specializing in Theoretical Physics, at the University of Science, Vietnam National University - Ho Chi Minh city. My thesis considered a scenario of neutrinos with dynamically varying mass in order to explain Dark Energy equation of state.
