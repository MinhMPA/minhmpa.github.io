PNG probes inflaton interactions and extra fields

The primordial bispectrum \(\langle\zeta\zeta\zeta\rangle=B(k_1,k_2,k_3)\) carries information about additional interactions and degrees of freedom during inflation.

squeezedwhat else was around: extra light fields (local), or a massive particle (cosmological collider)
equilateralhow it interacts: self-coupling (intrinsic, or induced by heavy fields), reduced \(c_s\)

\(f_{\rm NL}\) is the amplitude of a PNG shape/template.

Opening (~1 min). The power spectrum is the free-field two-point function; non-Gaussianity is the first probe of the inflaton's interactions and of any extra fields present — cosmology's closest analog of a scattering amplitude [Maldacena 2002; EFT of inflation; Green TASI 2212.08685, §5.2–5.3]. The shape carries the physics: the squeezed limit reads the field content (extra light fields give local; a massive field m~H leaves an oscillatory collider signal in the squeezed limit, Green Eq. 5.27), the equilateral shape reads the inflaton self-interactions / reduced c_s — INCLUDING self-interactions induced by integrating out heavy (m≫H) fields; Λ ↔ the mass gap, and even DBI's small c_s descends from a UV completion [Green TASI §5.3]. The dictionary's split is by mass: fields dynamical at horizon crossing (m≲H) show up in the squeezed limit; fields heavy enough to integrate out show up as equilateral self-couplings. Orthogonal is the third standard template but carries no cleanly distinct physics, so I leave it off. Don't define f_NL — the room knows it. Sets up local and equilateral for the next slide.

Why target primordial non-Gaussianity at unity?

Local · consistency-relation null test

\[ f_{\rm NL}^{\rm loc}=\tfrac{5}{12}\,(1-n_s)\simeq 1.5\times10^{-2} \]

single-clock + adiabatic + attractor

\( \sigma\!\left(f_{\rm NL}^{\rm loc}\right)\lesssim 1 \)

  • A measured \(|f_{\rm NL}^{\rm loc}|\sim\mathcal{O}(1)\) would mean an extra field, nonlinear superhorizon evolution, or a non-attractor phase.
  • \(n_s\) sets where the relation becomes testable.

Rules out standard attractor single-clock dynamics, not literally every one-field model [Green, TASI 2212.08685 §5.2].

Equilateral · EFT naturalness test

\[ f_{\rm NL}^{\rm equil}\sim\mathcal{O}(1)\!\left[\,c_s^{-2}\!-\!1,\ \tfrac{\dot\phi^{2}}{\Lambda^4},\ \ldots\right] \]

\(c_s\) = sound speed of the fluctuations  ·  \(\dot\phi\) = inflaton velocity  ·  \(\Lambda\) = EFT cutoff of inflation

\( \bigl|f_{\rm NL}^{\rm equil}\bigr|\sim 1\!-\!10,\ \ \sigma\!\left(f_{\rm NL}^{\rm equil}\right)\lesssim 1 \)

  • Canonical slow-roll gives \(f_{\rm NL}^{\rm eq}\ll1\) (weakly coupled). A measured \(f_{\rm NL}^{\rm eq}>1\) rules that out.
  • Unity asks a different question: is the inflaton weakly coupled?

\(1\!-\!10\) is a natural regime, not a universal prediction; pure Einstein-gravity terms stay slow-roll suppressed. A heavy field \(m\sim H\) gives an orthogonal / quasi-single-field shape instead [Green, TASI 2212.08685 §5.3].

Same target, two different questions: local asks whether the single-clock consistency relation held; equilateral asks how strongly the adiabatic mode self-interacts.
Theorist hook (~1.5 min). LEFT: (5/12)(1−n_s)≈0.015 is the single-clock/adiabatic/attractor NULL prediction — NOT an observable LSS floor (the physical squeezed bispectrum for single-field is a gauge/coordinate mode, cancelled in conformal-Fermi frame [Pajer, Schmidt, Zaldarriaga; Tanaka-Urakawa; Cabass+]). Unity is where consistency-relation violations (extra clock/multifield, curvaton-type superhorizon nonlinearity, ultra-slow-roll/non-attractor) become detectable — n_s doesn't predict it. RIGHT — the expert-proof chain, each step citable: (1) f_π⁴ ≡ 2M_Pl²|Ḣ|c_s (=φ̇² at c_s=1), the Goldstone normalization [Cheung+ 0709.0293]. (2) A_s = H⁴/(4π²f_π⁴); Planck A_s=2.1e−9 ⇒ f_π/H = (4π²A_s)^{−1/4} ≈ 59 [Green TASI §5.3]. NB: a DIMENSIONLESS ratio — H itself unknown (only r<0.036 bounds it); "why f_π≫H" = "why fluctuations are 10⁻⁵", same fact. Assumes ζ is the single-clock Goldstone. (3) f_NL^eq·Δ_ζ ∝ H²/Λ² [Green TASI], so PARAMETRICALLY f_NL^eq ~ (f_π/Λ)² — never quote as equality. (4) Weak coupling (Λ≫f_π) ⇒ f_NL^eq≪1; canonical slow-roll gives f_NL ~ O(ε,η)~10⁻² [Maldacena 0210603]. (5) Green verbatim: "any measurement of f_NL^eq>1 would exclude canonical slow-roll"; the converse (f_NL^eq≪1 ⟺ weakly coupled fundamental scalar) is a CONJECTURE — say so. (6) Consistency requires only the strong-coupling scale above H (predictive at freeze-out); f_π≈59H leaves the window H≲Λ≲f_π ⟺ f_NL^eq≳1 = "new physics on the horizon" [Baumann and Green 1102.5343]. (7) SAY the scale statement (removed from the slide as opaque): the φ̇²/Λ⁴ entry hits 1 when Λ ~ φ̇^{1/2} = (2ε)^{1/4}√(HM_Pl) — and φ̇^{1/2} IS f_π (c_s=1), ≈59H. So unity means "the cutoff has come down to the symmetry-breaking scale", parked at the geometric mean of H and M_Pl (up to ε^{1/4}). (8) Planck bound: c_s ≥ 0.021 (95%) [1905.05697]. If pushed on exact Λ_U(c_s) scalings: the c_s-power depends on which cubic operator dominates — do NOT quote a power from memory; point to Baumann–Green. Caveats: equilateral template ≠ all heavy-field signals (m~H → orthogonal/QSFI/collider shapes); f_NL=1 is not a sharp wall — slow-roll sits at 10⁻², the 1–10 decade is where natural strongly-coupled models live. If asked "self-interactions ONLY?": no — couplings to m≫H fields, once those are integrated out, GENERATE π self-interactions (Λ ↔ the mass gap; even DBI's small c_s descends from its UV completion) [Green TASI §5.3]. Equilateral detects that heavy physics coupled to the inflaton exists at Λ≲f_π, but cannot separate intrinsic from induced — that degeneracy is the defining property of an EFT. (Heavy exchange also leaves an exponentially suppressed consistency-relation violation in the squeezed limit; the S/N sits in the equilateral part.)

Galaxies respond to the long-wavelength density mode

Peak-background split: a long density mode \(\delta_L\) lifts the local background, so more peaks cross \(\delta_{\rm cr}\) and more galaxies form.

\[ \delta_g = b_1\,\delta_m \]

\(b_1\) = how galaxy abundance responds to the long density mode. Local PNG adds a second response: the long potential mode also modulates the small-scale power.

The bridge to dirt. Galaxies form above a collapse threshold δ_cr; a long density mode δ_L raises the background so more short-scale peaks cross → δ_g = b₁ δ_m. b₁ = the peak-background-split response to the long DENSITY mode. Tee up next: local PNG makes the long POTENTIAL mode modulate the small-scale variance (σ₈ local), so galaxies respond to that too → a second response b_φ → scale-dependent bias. Galaxy abundance responds to both the density and the potential mode

\[ b_1=\frac{d\ln\bar n}{d\ln\bar\rho_m}\,,\qquad\qquad b_\phi=2\,\frac{d\ln\bar n}{d\ln\sigma_8} \]

\(b_1\): how the galaxy number density \(\bar n\) responds to a long mode of density fluctuations.    \(b_\phi\): how \(\bar n\) responds to \(\sigma_8\), the amplitude of density fluctuations, rescaled by local PNG and the long potential mode.

\[ \text{universal mass function}\;+\;\text{tracer conservation}\;\;\Longrightarrow\;\; b_\phi=2\delta_c\,(b_1-1) \]

The usual shortcut for \(b_\phi\). See Part II for why it might fail for observed galaxies.

Formal definitions (mirrors the AIS talk, page 5). b1 = d ln n̄/d ln ρ̄_m is the linear density bias — response of the abundance to the long density mode. b_φ = 2 d ln n̄/d ln σ8 is the PNG bias — local PNG modulates the small-scale amplitude σ8 through the long potential mode, so the abundance responds via σ8. From a universal mass function n(ν), ν=δ_c/σ, plus tracer conservation, the two are tied: b_φ = 2δ_c(b1−1). That tie is the assumption Part II attacks (non-universality: b_φ = 2δ_c(b1−p), p≠1 for real galaxies). These are the same b1, b_φ used for galaxy sizes in Part I. ~1 min.

Nothing in late-time physics mimics the \(k^{-2}\) bias

\[ \Delta b(k)\;\propto\; b_\phi\,f_{\rm NL}\,k^{\,\Delta-2} \]

local \(k^{-2}\)  ·  orthogonal \(k^{-1}\)  ·  equilateral \(k^{0}\)

\(\Delta\) is the squeezed-limit exponent of the primordial bispectrum.

First theorist hook — deliver the collider line to the correlator people specifically. ~1.5 min. Detail to say: Δb ∝ b_φ f_NL / M(k), M∝k²T(k). Scalings from the squeezed-limit exponent Δ: local Δ=0 → k⁻² (steep low-k divergence, nothing in late-time physics mimics it [Dalal+ 2008]); orthogonal Δ=1 → k⁻¹; equilateral Δ=2 → k⁰ (degenerate with ordinary bias b₁). For massive spinning particles the exponent becomes intermediate/oscillatory.

Reaching \(\sigma(f_{\rm NL})\approx1\) means clearing three bottlenecks

Too few modes. The local \(k^{-2}\) signal sits at the lowest \(k\), where even a 3D survey runs short. Part I adds a second observable.

The \(b_\phi\) degeneracy. Biased tracers give only \(b_\phi f_{\rm NL}\), and the universality shortcut \(b_\phi=2\delta_c(b_1-1)\) fails for real galaxies. Part II pins \(b_\phi\) from data.

A lossy statistic. The power spectrum throws away higher-order information. Part III keeps the whole field.

The spine, in words. Bottleneck 1 (too few modes): a statistical floor, local-specific — the k⁻² signal lives at low k. Bottleneck 2 (b_φ degeneracy): a systematic floor, generic — we only ever measure b_φ f_NL, and universality b_φ=2δ_c(b₁−1) fails for the stellar-mass/colour/flux samples surveys observe (b_φ ≈ 2 ∂ln n̄/∂ln σ8). Bottleneck 3 (lossy statistic): an information floor, generic — P(k) discards higher-order/mode-coupling info. Each part takes one on, and they climb in non-linearity (linear 2-pt, then small scales, then the full field) — the workshop theme. ~1 min.

Galaxy sizes as LSS tracers

\[ I_{ij}\propto\sum_p w_p\,(x_{i,p}-x_{i,g})(x_{j,p}-x_{j,g}) \]

size \(=\) trace \(t\equiv\mathrm{tr}\,I_{ij}\);   field \(T(\mathbf{x})=\sum_g t\,\delta_D(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{x}_g)\)

\[ \delta_T=\frac{T}{\bar T}-1,\qquad \boxed{\;\delta_s \equiv \delta_T-\delta_g\;}=b_s\,\delta_m \]

Same 2-point formalism as counts, on galaxies you already have.

Trace/size definition (paper Eq. 3, 5–8). Each halo's size = the trace of its (mass-weighted) second-moment inertia tensor, t = tr I_ij = Σ_p w_p |x_p − x_g|²; trace field T(x)=Σ_g t δ_D(x−x_g), mean T̄=n̄⟨t⟩, δ_T=T/T̄−1. Subtract the count field to isolate the SIZE perturbation: δ_s ≡ δ_T − δ_g = b_s δ_m — same linear-bias, 2-point formalism as counts (P_ss = b_s² P_mm + P_shot). w_p = radial weight (reduced tensor w_p ∝ r_p⁻²). Information already in imaging; cheap. ~1.5 min.

Part III · New Statistics

Field-level inference

Compress to moments, or keep the field?

A few bins of Fourier wavenumber k  —vs—  every Fourier wavector \(\mathbf k\).

Your own LeCosPa visuals. Set up the contrast; the next slide makes it an equation. ~1 min.

Where do the initial conditions go?

\[ \mathcal{P}(\theta\mid d)\;\propto\;\mathcal{P}(\theta)\!\int\!\mathcal{D}\delta_{\rm in}\;\mathcal{P}(d\mid\delta_{\rm in},\theta)\,\mathcal{P}(\delta_{\rm in}\mid\theta) \]

one marginalization over the ICs · shared prior \(\mathcal{P}(\delta_{\rm in}\mid\theta)\), where \(f_{\rm NL}\) lives · the only difference is the data \(d\)

SUMMARY  ·  \(d=\widehat P_k=\tfrac{1}{N_k}\!\sum_{\mathbf k\in\,\rm bin}|\delta(\mathbf k)|^2\)

\[ \ln\mathcal{P}(\widehat P\mid\theta)=-\tfrac12\!\!\sum_{i\,\in\,\text{bins}}\!\frac{\big[\widehat P_{k_i}-P_{k_i}(\theta)\big]^2}{\sigma^2_{k_i}(\theta)} \]

average \(|\delta(\mathbf k)|^2\) in each bin, then sum over bins

\(\sigma^2_{k_i}(\theta)\) = sample (cosmic) variance + shot noise · phases gone

FIELD LEVEL  ·  \(d=\delta(\mathbf k)\)

\[ \ln\mathcal{P}(\delta\mid\delta_{\rm in},\theta)=-\tfrac12\!\sum_{\mathbf k}\frac{\big|\delta^{\rm obs}(\mathbf k)-\delta^{\rm det}(\mathbf k;\theta,\delta_{\rm in})\big|^2}{\sigma_\epsilon^2(\mathbf k)} \]

compare each mode, then sum over modes

\(\sigma_\epsilon^2(\mathbf k)\) = free EFT noise, not Poisson [eq 5] · sample \(\delta_{\rm in}\) · phases kept

A handful of bandpowers  vs  every mode — that gap is the compression.

THE theorist slide — one Bayesian posterior, two choices of data d, same −½Σ(residual)²/variance form. SUMMARY (d=P̂): average |δ_k|² over the modes IN EACH BIN to form the bandpower P̂(k_i), then SUM OVER BINS — a handful of bandpowers. Gaussian bandpower variance σ²_{k_i}(θ) = (2/N_modes)[P_{k_i}(θ)+1/n̄]² = sample(=cosmic) variance + shot noise — θ-dependent, so strictly there's also a −½Σ ln σ²(θ) (ln det C) term, usually dropped by fixing the covariance at fiducial (also avoids param-dependent-covariance bias); the sample-variance part IS the IC integral done analytically. NB: P = galaxy auto-power P_gg (NOT linear matter); relax Poisson → 1/n̄ becomes σ_ε²(k) (free EFT stochastic noise, scale-dep, paper eq 3), so σ²_{k_i}=2[P_gg+σ_ε²]²/N_modes. FIELD-LEVEL (d=δ): compare EACH MODE δ^obs(k) to the deterministic δ^det(k;θ,δ_in) = Σ_O b_O O[α,ŝ] (bias expansion on ŝ=δ_in), SUM OVER MODES, marginalize δ_in by sampling. Denominator σ_ε²(k) = paper eq (5) noise = same stochastic noise as the summary's shot term. CAREFUL (do NOT claim FLI beats cosmic variance): σ_ε² is the CONDITIONAL noise given δ_in — no cosmic variance THERE — but the PARAMETER posterior marginalizes the unknown δ_in, inferred from ONE realization, which DOES carry cosmic variance. For a Gaussian field, marginalized FLI ≡ the P(k) likelihood exactly (cov 2[P_gg+σ_ε²]²/N); FLI does NOT beat cosmic variance for Gaussian modes (P(k) is sufficient). The 3.5–5.2× gain in 2403.03220 is NON-GAUSSIAN info (nonlinear gravity + PNG) that P(k) discards — for a Gaussian field the factor is 1. Genuine sample-variance cancellation needs specific handles: multitracer (Part I) or nonlinear reconstruction. (Eq 5 keeps ln 2πσ_ε² since σ_ε² is free.) Kept phases = non-Gaussian info → summary sufficient only for a Gaussian field (next slide). f_NL sits in the shared prior 𝒫(δ_in|θ). ~2 min — slow down.
