## VLLT Joint Seminar Series on Physics and Astronomy

The idea started during the pandemic, when seminars moved online.

I put together a group of Vietnamese junior postdocs and graduate students in physics and astrophysics to organize this virtual seminar series in [2021](https://vllt-joint-seminar-series.github.io/2021/) and [2022](https://vllt-joint-seminar-series.github.io/2022/) for undergraduate and early graduate students in Vietnam. The goal was to expose students to state-of-the-art research in physics and astronomy, the kind of exposure I wish I had during my undergraduate days.

We have hosted many young international and Vietnamese researchers as speakers, and we plan to continue doing so.

## Science Communication Fellows

As a [Science Communication Fellow](https://lsa.umich.edu/ummnh/u-m-community/u-m-faculty/science-communication-fellows.html) at the University of Michigan Museum of Natural History, I perform science demonstrations during their [Scientist Spotlight](https://lsa.umich.edu/ummnh/visitors/things-to-do/scientist-spotlight.html) and science presentations during their [Science Forum](https://lsa.umich.edu/ummnh/visitors/museumathome/expert-expo/science-forum.html) events.

## Skype a Scientist

<p>
  <img src="/images/Skype_a_Scientist.jpg" alt="Skype a Scientist" style="float:right; max-width:320px; width:100%; margin:0 0 1rem 1rem;" />
  Skype a Scientist is a non-profit international program that connects science classrooms with active researchers. Through informal interviews and conversations, students learn more about science, scientific research, and what it is like to work as a scientist. If you are a scientist or a science teacher, I strongly encourage you to consider <a href="https://www.skypeascientist.com/sign-up.html">signing up</a>.
</p>

## Physicists To-Go

This is similar to Skype a Scientist, but managed by the American Physical Society. If you are in the United States, consider signing up [here](https://www.aps.org/programs/outreach/physiciststogo.cfm).
